Rosey Grier Biography

Cuthbert native Rosey Grier is an American actor, singer, Protestant minister, and former professional American football player. Rosey started playing football at an early age.

After his retirement, Grier hosted the Rosey Grier Show on KABC-TV, a weekly half-hour television show discussing community affairs in Los Angeles.

Grier was appointed as a bodyguard for his pal, United States senator and presidential candidate Robert F. Kennedy. He was the guard of Ethel Kennedy, the Senator’s wife. She was then expecting a child, the night that Kennedy was assassinated in Los Angeles in 1968.

Grier and Olympic decathlon gold medalist Rafer Johnson heard shots fired ahead of them. Grier came close and saw men wrestling with gunman Sirhan Sirhan. Grier jumped into the fray.

Rosey Grier Education

He played high school football at Abraham Clark High School in Roselle, New Jersey, graduating in 1951. Grier attended Pennsylvania State University, 1951-55.

He was a great college football participant for The Pennsylvania State University. The university was named among the National Collegiate Athletic Association 100th anniversary list of 100 most influential student-athletes.

As a professional player, Grier was selected to be part of the New York Giants and the original Fearsome Foursome of the Los Angeles Rams. He participated in the Pro Bowl twice.

Rosey Grier Professional Football

Grier’s professional career started in 1955 at the age of 23. He became the University’s star footballer. He also joined ‘Alpha Phi Alpha’ the first-ever Greek-lettered fraternity, established for African-American students.

In 1955, Rosey was picked to play for ‘New York Giants’ (1995 NFL Draft). He continued playing for the ‘Giants’ until 1962.
During his time with the ‘Giants’, he gave notable performances and played a major role in winning ‘NFL Championship’ in 1956 and ‘Eastern Conference Championships’ in 1958, 1959, 1961, and 1962.

While he was still playing for ‘New York Giants’, he was picked for the ‘Pro Bowl’ first in 1956, and then 1961. His defensive game earned him the title ‘All-Pro’ in 1956.

He was again bestowed with the title from 1958-62. During this phase of his career, many other teams showed their interest in drafting him into their respective side and in 1963 he was eventually selected to play for ‘Los Angeles Rams’.

As a professional footballer, he played for ‘Rams’ during the final stages of his career. Grier became a part of the strongest defensive line in the game of football. Along with other star players like Deacon Jones, Merlin Olsen, and Lamar Lundy, the defensive line was popularly known as ‘The Fearsome Foursome’. His football career ended in 1967 after he couldn’t recover completely from a ruptured Achilles Tendon.

Rosey Grier Awards and Achievements

He was honored by Pennsylvania State University with a Distinguished Alumni Award in 1974. Rosey also received Alumni Fellow Award in 1991. His name was included among ‘100 Most Influential Student-Athletes’ by NCAA at its 100th anniversary. Rosey was inducted into the New Jersey’s Sports Hall of Fame in 1997.

Grier was again inducted into New Jersey Hall of Fame in 2017. His other accolades include ‘Master of Ceremonies for the Special Olympics’ (1970 – 83), ‘Watson Award’ from IBM, ‘B’nai B’rith Champion of Youth’, ‘National Football League Humanitarian Award’, etc. He was also awarded an honorary Doctorate of Letter of Law from Orals Roberts University in 1981.

Rosey Grier Other Work

Grier is the co-founder of ‘American Neighbourhood Enterprises’ (ANE). The organization’s purpose is to help the homeless by providing them with shelter and training them to keep up with life. Grier was selected as a Protestant minister in 1983. He then created another program called ‘Rosey Grier’s Are You Committed?’

The second program was meant to help disadvantaged teenagers and young people. His vision was to provide the young with educational and spiritual values. He also held a position as the chairman of ‘Impact Urban America’ – a branch of ANE.

He is also one of the board members of ‘Impact Urban America’ which uses real estate and construction business. This was to provide training, employment and affordable housing to the underprivileged. Rosey is also a trustee in the ‘Milken Family Foundation’ which provides for the medical research, social welfare and advancement of the society as a whole.

Grier has also devoted his time to ‘The Prostate Cancer Foundation’. He is the writer of several books – ‘Shooting Star’ (1993), ‘Rosey Grier’s Needlepoint for Men’ (1973), ‘Rosey Grier’s All-American Heroes: Multicultural Success Stories’ (1993), etc. His autobiography ‘Rosey, an Autobiography: The Gentle Giant’ was released in 1986. On January 5, 2017. Grier reported that he would be seeking to be the Governor of California from the Republican Party during the California Gubernatorial Election in 2018. But he removed himself from the candidacy in July 2017.

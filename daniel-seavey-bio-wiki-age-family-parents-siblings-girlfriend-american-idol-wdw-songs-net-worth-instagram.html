Who is Daniel Seavey? Biography and Wiki

Born on April 2, 1999, Vancouver, Washington, the USA to Jeffrey Seavey and Keri Seavey. Daniel Seavey is a young  American musician best known as a member of a pop boy band “Why Don’t We”(WDW).

He came limelight as the contestant of “American Idol” show at the age of 16. He made it through in the  14th season audition of the show with three yeses from the judges one of them being Jennifer Lopez who valued Daniel’s talent and hoped to see him perform again.

He, however, held just the 9th place, but gain experience. He plays nine musical instruments: the guitar, piano, cello, recorder, saxophone, drums, violin, viola, and bass. He would perform on the streets of Portland for the art walks and his Dad would be with him throughout the traveling adventures. He built his solo career performances on the American Idol competition and his features in other artists’ songs.

Daniel Seavey American Idol

Daniel auditioned for the fourteenth season of the singing competition TV series, American Idol in January 2015 at the age of 15, making him the youngest contestant. His auditioning performance included singing Leonard Cohen’s “Hallelujah” & Paula Abdul’s “Straight Up” in San Francisco.

He made it to the top nine when he was eliminated on April 1st. He played the following instruments: ukulele, violin, cello, guitar, viola, piano, mandolin, bass, and drums. He performed the songs: “Thinking Out Loud” by Ed Sheeran for his first solo performance, “Straight Up” in the House of Blues for his spot in the final 24, and “I See Fire” for his final solo performance.

Daniel Seavey Why Don’t We (WDW)

WDW is an American boy band consisting of five members, Zach Herron, Jack Avery, Daniel Seavey, Corbyn Besson, and Jonah Marais, the band was formed on September 27, 2016. Each of the members had solo careers prior to joining the group. The band released their first single, “Taking You,” in October 2016. Their first live show was in December 2016. The band has over 4.5 million followers on their whydontwemusic Instagram. They won the 2019 Teen Choice Award for Choice Music Group.

Post Malone Biography

Post Malone is an American rapper, singer, songwriter, and record producer, who is best known by his stage name Post Malone, he is also known for his introspective songwriting and laconic vocal style, he gained acclaim for bending a range of genres including hip hop, pop, country, and rock. He first became famous back in 2015 following the release of his debut single “White Iverson”.

Post Malone Music Syles

Post’s music is mainly hip hop and pop, while he incorporates elements of trap, pop-rap, country, rock, R&B and cloud rap. His music has been described as a “melting pot of the country, grunge, hip-hop, and R&B” and he himself has been described as versatile in his vocal style and he has also been described as laconic.

Jon Caramanica of The New York Times described Malone as “an artist who toes the line between singing, rapping, hip-hop and spooky electric folk”. Malone himself called his music “genre-less”. He cites Bob Dylan, who became interested around the age of 15, as an influence on his music, calling him “a genius” and “a god” though his music has been called “as far away from Rock n’ Roll as you can get.”

He called “Subterranean Homesick Blues” the “first rap song”. He has also cited Kurt Cobain and Johnny Cash as key influences too. He has also cited 50 Cent, whom he called a legend, Kanye West and Key! as influences.

Post Malone Singer and Songwriter

He began his professional music when he was in a heavy metal band. His debut album Stoney (2016), was trap and cloud rap focused, which was a commercial success that featured the hit single “Congratulations”. In 2018, the album broke the record in most of the weeks on the US Billboard Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums chart after it had reached its 77th week on the chart.

His second album Beerbongs & Bentleys (2018) debuted number one on the Billboard 200 and broke several streaming records upon its release. In featuring the Billboard Hot 100 number-one singles “Rockstar” and “Psycho”, were nominated for Album of the Year during the 2018 Grammy Awards.

In 2018, he collaborated with Swae Lee on the song “Sunflower” for the soundtrack to the film Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse, which reached the top of the Billboard Hot 100. He later inaugurated his own festival, Posty Fest, which took place at Dos Equis Pavilion on October 28 and that featured Travis Scott alongside him as the headliners.

His third album Hollywood’s Bleeding, was released in 2019, explored indie pop and rock. It then became his second number-one album on the Billboard 200 and featured the Billboard Hot 100 number-one single “Circles”. He has sold over 65 million records in the US alone.

He, therefore, received a Diamond certification by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA) for “Congratulations”. His accolades include three American Music Awards, a Billboard Music Award, and an MTV Video Music Award. Additionally, he has also received six Grammy Award nominations during his career.

Post Malone Political views

He has a tattoo of U.S. president John F. Kennedy on his arm, whereby he explained by saying that Kennedy was “the only President to speak out against the crazy corruption stuff that’s going on in our government nowadays.”

In December 2016, he stated that if he would be asked to perform at the inauguration of Donald Trump he would not have been opposed, though he stated that neither Donald Trump nor Hillary Clinton was fit to be President of the United States and he also expressed his support of Bernie Sanders during the election cycle.

He later expressed a dislike for Trump in November 2017 interview for Rolling Stone. In the same interview, he revealed that he has a large collection of guns and he also believes it is an American right to own and operate guns. He also expressed a strong interest in conspiracy theories when he said, “There’s crazy shit that goes on that we can’t explain.”

During his trip to Canada, he was interviewed and when he was asked what is the “biggest lie is”, he replied “The biggest lie in the world is the U.S. government. It’s a fucking reality show and I think there’s gonna be a lot of weird shit that happens within our generation that really changes the way of the world.”

Post Malone Controversy

Post has been called a “culture vulture” multiple times by different publications and on social media for “appropriating” African-American culture. California rapper Lil B wrote on Twitter in October 2017: that “Post Malone is slowly turning into a white dude! Lol, he’s pushing it, give it a few years he gon be full country and hate blacks lol”, with Post replying in an interview, claiming that his white skin has been “used against him.”

In January 2018, he went on an intoxicated rant against people who describe him as a culture vulture. In his interview with GQ that is several days later, he said: “there’s a struggle being a white rapper.” In his interview in November 2017 with Polish media outlet NewOnce, Malone said that modern hip-hop music lacks “people talking about real shit” and added that “if you’re looking to think about life, don’t listen to hip-hop.”

He received backlash comments from his social media, from fellow rappers such as Lil B and Vince Staples. He later appeared in a video on Twitter, saying that the reason for his comments was that it was a “beer-tasting” interview, and he went on and said that he loves hip-hop. NewOnce, however, denied that claim, stating that Malone barely drank at all during the interview.
